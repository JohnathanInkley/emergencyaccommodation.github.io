Dear Paul Cooper and Cllr Ahmed,

I am a member of the London Renters Union, a group of over 7000 people across London who support each other with housing problems through legal action, media work and protest.

I am writing regarding the case of one of my fellow members Nurjahan Akter. She is due to be evicted by bailiffs next Monday, with her young children. She completed a homelessness application to Redbridge Council and followed all the steps the council requires. And yet it is now less than a week until she is due to be evicted and she has not been offered a safe place by the council for her family in the borough.

I am writing to you to ask you to step in and ensure the council provide a place for her by Friday 13th March so that she and her family can move in over the weekend. Local residents will not sit idly by and watch families be forced from their homes and young children thrown out onto the street. Do you think this is acceptable?

Please reply to me and the London Renters Union (cc’ed) letting us know when you have found a place for Nurjahan and her family in the borough.

Yours sincerely,
